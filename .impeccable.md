## Design Context

### Users
Primary users for this lab prototype are neuroscience researchers, students, and scientifically curious readers exploring how salience-driven switching across SN, FPN, and DMN can be narrated in an accessible browser interface. Their main job is to inspect switching behavior over time without needing to handle raw imaging files in the browser.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel cerebral, exploratory, and alive. It should communicate scientific seriousness without reading like a sterile clinical dashboard, and it should preserve a sense of curiosity and play while explaining complex network dynamics.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a warm, editorial instrument-panel aesthetic rather than default dark-mode sci-fi. Favor expressive serif display type, precise sans-serif body text, softly tinted scientific colors, and motion that helps users perceive state changes. Avoid generic SaaS cards, neon glows, and purple-blue AI gradients.

### Design Principles
1. Treat the browser as a playback and inquiry console, not a preprocessing engine.
2. Make state transitions legible at a glance through motion, color, and compact metrics.
3. Keep the experience exploratory and teachable rather than clinically opaque.
4. Design for progressive disclosure so synthetic playback can evolve into real cohort analysis.
5. Preserve scientific clarity while allowing the interface to feel intentional and memorable.
